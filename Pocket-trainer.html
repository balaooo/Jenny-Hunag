This is a term project that me and my other 7 teammates designed for Human Computer Interaction course in National Taiwan University during 2019. I’m in charge of user research, user story and lo-fi prototype. We aimed to build an AR function along with an app for users to interact with during learning exercise.

Problem focus

AR, Learning, Sports.

In first stage of exploration, we decided that our project will be focusing on learning using AR technology. Under the scope of learning, there’s actually lots of different topics, for example language, cooking, and sports etc. After several conversation, we converge our topic into sports. Especially in college, only 6 credits of sports course are required in normal curriculum. Extra sports teaching materials without adding students’ burden becomes an important issue.

Target users & background

Student, Fresh graduates, Shy people.

Since in our problem focus stage, we decided to do it under the scope of learning, so students are our main target user. We still tried to explore other groups of users by affinity diagram, and got a conclusion that with the idea of learning exercise alone, it could also help young lady with mediocre incomes, shy people, and someone who cares about body figure but lack of motivation. Also, location will be focused on both school gyms and local gyms.

Our discussion process

Define research problem

After we defined our target users, we quickly listed out all the questions and categorized them into several groups.

  • Do you workout regularly? If yes, how often?
  • Where do you usually go workout and why?
  • What kind of feelings do you have when you go workout by yourself?
  • Do your economic status affect your workout habit?
  • What makes you start to exercise?
  • What’s your opinion on monthly fee of the gym?
  • Do you attend personal training class, why or why not?
  • Do your body image affect your wiilingness of working out?

Problem statement

How to learn exercise with instruction but without extra cost?

User Journey

In order to understand how to use an equipment, but not paying high priced personal training course, users would consult a friend or memorize the name of the equipment and searched it on the internet. But even with these efforts, users often felt confused if they were using the equipment correctly.

Design and solution

Use app to choose workout and follow instruction in AR glasses

With all of the information above, we decided to use AR glasses alone with app to solve this problem. So that users can learn exercise from the AR glasses by themselves without spending time searching online or asking friends. Meanwhile, AR trainer can show users how to adjust their position in the glasses, so users can do the exercise correctly without injuring themselves.

Video mockup

After setting up in the app, you will begin your workout journey as the video indicated.

Business scope

We expect this AR glasses can be placed in school gym, so students don’t have to pay for personal training course, but still learn workout in correct way. Also, normal gyms can buy this product, to lower down the cost of hiring an actual trainer.

Results

We tested 12 users in total, and here were some feedbacks to consider when building hi-fi prototype

  • The glasses might become slippery when sweating during exercise.
  • It’s easy to understand, and you can get feedback immediately. Love the privacy when working out.
  • Adding some more instruction when the arrow appears will lead less confusion
  • Adding cool down lesson
