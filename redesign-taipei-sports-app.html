This is a side project I did on Jan-Feb 2022, aiming for avoiding unhappy workout experience due to the crowdedness in public sport center or local gym. I am responsible for UX research, UX design, and UI design. After several user tests and gathering feedback, the second version was revised in Jun 2022.

Why this project?

This is a typical senario that I discovered after user testing and a quote was told during interview:

"Even though I checked live stream on the app before heading out, the gym still get packed before I get there or while I’m exercising. This makes me feel uncomfortable." --The energetic man

Insights

Even though with the curent app, it's still hard for users to anticipate the crowdedness of the gym.

User research shows that 80% of users who care about quality of working out will always go on the app or website of the fitness center to check current number of people. However, it doesn’t help that much because even knowing the number of people in the fitness center before heading to it, there's still a chance that the venue will become crowded after arriving. Therefore, the main design goal is to give more clues and information to users on the app, so they can make decision based on these facts and create a much more enjoyable workout experience.

Problem statement

How do users avoid crowdedness of the gym by using the app?

We can inspect the current user flow and find out that now users don’t interact with the app for a long time. Also, the information placed on the app are not enough for users to tell the crowdedness of the gym after they arrived at the gym.

Redesign features

  1. Chart of all-day stream, with highlight of current number of people: Users can easily tell its crowdedness before and during their workout when they are at home.
  2. Switching day with a click: If it’s crowded, then users can check data from the other day, and seek for another proper time slot.
  3. Crowdedness highlight tag: Compare to numbers, having a tag with color beside sports provide a more specific visual clue for users to decide whether going workout or not.
  4. A hint of average staying time: For example, bouldering takes 2-3 hours; however, gym session normally only needs 1-1.5 hours. This is a second clue for users to estimate the crowdedness of the venue.

User testing

Because it’s impractical for me to acquire real data from each sports center, so my user testing focus more on the fluency of the flow and try to know if there’s any confusing elements.

  • Please check the current number of people in Beitou swimming pool.
  • If it’s crowded now in the pool, and you aren’t willing to go, what will you do next?

Results & takeaways

With this solution, 5 out of 5 users responded they feel much more relief after seeing the statistics. If their time is flexible, they will based on the past information and decide when to go workout, so they can get a comfortable experience. However, couple of feedbacks need to be considered afterward:

  • Day selector order: 2 users feel that re-adjusting the day order after they choose will lead some confusions. It will be better if the order stays at the same place no matter which day is it.
  • Swipe to change date: 3 users try to swipe to change different date instead of using day selector. Adding swiping function will add on the over all user experience.
